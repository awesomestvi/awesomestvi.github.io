About Us
450 B.C.
In about 450 B.C, Hippocrates' theory of Temperament proposed the existence of four personality types, that each encompass a fixed set of inborn, intrinsic characteristics.
1921
Carl G. Jung brought this ancient understanding into popular consciousness through his book, "Psychological Types."
1956
The "Myers-Briggs Type Indicator" used Jung's and Hippocrates' four Temperments, and included Jung's theories of introversion and extraversion, to create sixteen "Types". The "MBTI", as it is known, is the most popular instrument in psychological typing across the world today.
