Communication Workshop

‘To effectively communicate, we must realize that we are all different in the way we perceive the world and use this understanding as a guide to our communication with others.’ -  Anthony Robbins

It’s common knowledge that effective communication is the key to success when working with others. The True Colors Communication workshop aims to facilitate this process by bringing forth different ideas and means for people to communicate with the different personality types. Each person learns to implement those techniques in ways which lead to effective communication with others. Further, you will discover how effective communication can considerably improve your personal performance at work. This hands-on, interactive workshop provides an opportunity for individuals to learn how to improve their individual communication skills and interact more effectively with different personality types. Also strengthens rapport, boost listening skills and clearly deliver their message in a mutually beneficial environment.

Key Benefits:

  • Learn to communicate using True Colors as your guide
  • Learn to make the communication process easier and more effective
  • Enhance personal performance through effective communication
Teaching & Learning Styles

Through the ‘Teaching & Learning Styles Certification Training’, participants are introduced to the True Colors concept in relation to personality and temperament styles. They engage in innovative and interactive activities which help build a framework for developing improved methods of teaching, bringing about greater communication in the classroom, and a positive school environment.  This workshop serves as a guide to educators to meet the needs of different students.  Teachers gather tools to help create an environment that is conducive to the students’ learning experience.  This certification training is particularly beneficial for alternative schools, drug abuse prevention, teaching life skills, and character development. The certification gives you the opportunity to deliver seminars, workshops, and in‐service programs and order True Colors facilitator‐only products. The Teaching & Learning Styles certification is a very specific training that employs the True Colors personality temperament model as the groundwork for integrating the Teaching & Learning Styles application throughout your organization.

Key Benefits:

  • Improve understanding of self and others and enhance the appreciation of differences
  • Provide renewal activities for teachers through positive professional development
  • Learn instructional strategies to increase student performance, support at risk learners and address needs of all students
  • Improve learning opportunities for all students and enhance communication between student, teacher, staff and parents
  • Create a more harmonious, organized classroom for each learning style
Conflict Resolution

According to True Colors, high functioning teams are made up of Gold, Green, Blue and Orange personality types. To work successfully with these types, it is vital that team leaders and members alike understand how to effectively cope with conflict.  There are those who are of the view that the only way to deal with conflict is to prevent it and others who believe that conflict is inevitable and can, in fact, help people grow.  In any case, successful teams will, at some point, be confronted with conflict within the group.

When it comes to handling challenging situations, how we choose to respond really is game.  When conflict arises, if we aren’t equipped with the skills and knowledge required to manage the situation, our emotions will take over, and the saying ‘there’s a time and a place for everything’ is equally true when it comes to using different conflict handling styles. There are basically five modes of managing conflict in any given situation. They are as follows -

The Competing mode is an appropriate response when you have to:

  • Take quick action
  • Make unpopular decisions
  • Stand up for vital issues.
  • The Collaborating Mode is an appropriate response for:
  • Learning
  • Merging Perspectives

The Compromising Mode is an appropriate approach for:

  • Resolving issues of Moderate importance
  • Reaching resolution with Equal Power and Strong Commitment, and
  • Creating Temporary Solutions

The Avoiding Mode is an appropriate response for:

  • Leaving unimportant issues alone
  • Reducing Tensions
  • Buying time
  • Knowing your limitations
  • Allowing other ownership
  • Recognizing issues as symptoms

The Accommodating Mode is an appropriate response for:

  • Leaving unimportant issues alone
  • Reducing Tensions
  • Buying time
  • Knowing your limitations
  • Allowing other ownership
  • Recognizing issues as symptoms

The Accommodating Mode is an appropriate response for:

  • Showing reasonableness
  • Developing performance
  • Creating goodwill
  • Keeping ‘Peace’
  • Retreating
  • Maintaining perspective

Time Management

“The bad news is time flies. The good news is you're the pilot." ― Michael Altshuler

How one manages their time depends on a variety of things, from the kind of lifestyle they lead, and the number of people and activities that occupy their time, to the "time habits" they have developed over the years. But that’s not all. The way you relate to time has much to do with your basic personality. If you have been through a True Colors workshop then you know we all have a blend of characteristics that make up our personality, many times with one of four groups of traits being most dominant. These personality characteristics have an impact on the way we manage our time

The True Colors workshop on Time Management helps you become aware of your strengths and stressors around time management and allows you to focus on solutions. Knowing about time types may help you realise why some people around you have such different ways of doing things. Instead of trying to get them to change, be aware of the differences and think about how to use each other’s strengths.
